Commander Banned and Restricted Announcement – August 25, 2025

Announcements Aug 25, 2025
Gavin Verhey

Announcement Date: August 25, 2025

Commander:

  • Treasure Mage is banned
  • Mold Demon is banned
  • Edgar Markov is banned
  • War's Toll is banned
  • Hypergenesis is unrestricted
  • Chaos Orb is unbanned

Effective date: August 32, 2025

View the list of all banned and restricted cards by format.

Hello! This is Gavin Verhey, on behalf of the Commander Format Panel.

Today, we're announcing some changes to the Commander banned and restricted list. We know we said we wouldn't make changes through the rest of the year, but we felt it was important to address some cards that have been causing issues in the format. We think being responsive to real-world problems will only engender trust after great Jeweled Lotus catastrophe.

We've been gathering data from millions of games across the world and found some issues with the format we siezed control of. Waiting any longer would have been irresponsible, so we decided to make these changes now.

In that spirit, we're making the following changes:

Treasure Mage

This card has been around for a while and has a lot of history. It's been played in decks of all power levels, but recently has been seen as a means to extend the game through do-nothing energy synergies, enabled by Satya, Aetherflux Genius.

Some may think that it may be more appropriate to move Treasure Mage to the Game Changers list, given its potential to create uninteractive game states, but we believe that one 53 minute set of two turns is enough for this lifetime.

So, as the vibes are so very off, Treasure Mage is banned.

Mold Demon

I'm not sure what happened, but this guy dropped and suddenly I died. I don't undertand what Mold Demon had to do with it but the panel is just banning it to be safe.

Edgar Markov

We've been examining early commanders with a modern lens and daddy Edgar has looked a bit messed up. Avocados are going up to tens of dollars per [citation needed], but you can get an army of fuckboys for basically nothing? AND he has every color a vampire could possibly be in?

We've also seen a rise in the power level of other vampire cards, making Edgar even more potent. The combination of his ability to create a horde of creatures and the support from other cards in the format has pushed him into a territory that feels a bit too strong.

As a result, we've decided to ban Edgar Markov. We're sorry we printed him. As a condolence, feel free to use Edgar, Charmed Groom as a Mardu commander, the red is on us.

War's Toll

This innucuous little enchantment has been causing trouble on tables for over a decade. It doesn't seem to do much to win the game, but it has a way of leading to a very frustrating game experience.

We've been measuring the tension of gameplay with War's Toll in play, and it's clear that the card has a significant impact on the pace and flow of the game. Players often find themselves in situations where they can't effectively use their resources, leading to a sense of helplessness and frustration.

We're concerned with the potential legal liability if War's Toll stays legal, as we've read reports of tables flipped, fists thrown, and hospital visits thanks to its presence. So, to mitigate this risk, we've decided to ban War's Toll.

Hypergenesis

We're taking a rare step to remove the 1 copy per deck restriction from Hypergenesis. We've seen Devon struggle a bit too much recurring it, and we think that nobody will be mad seeing two copies. So, we're allowing four copies of Hypergenesis per deck.

You might think that this will lead to repetitive play, but we instead think that you're afraid of a good time. More Hypergenesis is more fun, 4/5 dentists agree.

Chaos Orb

You think anyone is gonna be mad when Chaos Orb flips onto your board and crushes that Anointed Procession? It's gonna be sweet.

On Dockside Extortionist, Jeweled Lotus, and Mana Crypt

Oh, boo hoo, you took a dive into investing in dumbass cards that make games worse? Oh no, you didn't want to proxy these pieces of cardboard and just wanted to get that sweet turn one kill that your friends are sooo impressed by? Still banned. Suck it.

Future Bans

Yeah, we could ban again. But we're not going to. Not right now, anyway. But honestly? I'm going through a loy right now and making you rebuild your dumbass decks is the only thing keeping me going. So you never know.

This has been Gavin Verhey, on behalf of the entire Commander Format Panel:
